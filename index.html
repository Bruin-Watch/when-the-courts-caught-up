WHEN THE COURTS CAUGHT UP WITH THE LIES
Two Lawsuits UC Didn’t File. Neither Named the Retiree. Both Proved Him Right.
In 2023, UC sued its own technology contractor for a failed $28 million benefits system — the same system blamed when a retiree’s disability records vanished. Separately, the U.S. Department of Justice prosecuted UC’s insurance enrollment partner for $230 million in Medicare fraud. Two independent cases. Neither filed by the retiree. Both confirming what he documented years before the first subpoena was issued.

In 1992, a UCLA police officer bought a credit-card-sized Panasonic recorder, tucked it in his uniform pocket, and started documenting everything. They called him Inspector Gadget. He blew the whistle on command staff corruption. They fired the chief. Then they spent thirty-seven years trying to make the officer disappear.

This epilogue documents what happened when the courts finally looked at the same institution — and reached the same conclusions the motor cop had documented all along.
